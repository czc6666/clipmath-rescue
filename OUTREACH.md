## 拟发布文案

> I made a tiny, independent bookmarklet for this exact `data-formula` case: <https://czc6666.github.io/clipmath-rescue/>
>
> It does **not** replace Obsidian Web Clipper. Before clipping, it locally converts the current page's recognized `[data-formula]` SVG nodes into `$...$` / `$$...$$` text, then shows how many formulas were converted. Refreshing restores the page.
>
> I re-tested the URL from this issue today: it exposed 210 formula nodes, and the bookmarklet converted all 210 locally. It requests no extension permissions and sends no page URL, title, body, formulas, cookies, or tokens anywhere. Unknown formula shapes fail closed without changing the page.
>
> If you're still affected, could you try it on a non-sensitive article and report only: converted count, whether the clipped formulas rendered correctly, and which clipper you used? Please don't post article content or private URLs. A structured feedback form is linked on the page (GitHub login required).
>
> Source: <https://github.com/czc6666/clipmath-rescue>
